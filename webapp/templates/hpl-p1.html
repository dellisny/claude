HPL-P1 UNUSUAL OPTIONS SCREENER // S&P 500
WHAT IT DOES
Scans every S&P 500 ticker for options contracts where today's volume is unusually high relative to existing open interest — a sign that someone may be opening a new directional position. Survivors are cross-referenced with recent insider filings (SEC EDGAR) and congressional stock disclosures, then scored 0–10 by conviction.
RUNNING A SCAN
Press RUN SCAN. The scan runs in three phases: Scanning fetches options chains for ~500 tickers (takes 10–20 min), Enriching looks up insider and congressional trades for flagged tickers, then results are scored and saved. Past scans are listed in the sidebar and can be reloaded instantly.
HARD FILTERS (all must pass)
Vol / OI ≥ 2×Today's volume exceeds existing open interest — new positions being opened
Premium ≥ $250KFilters out retail noise; vol × price × 100 shares
DTE 7–21 daysNear-term expiry — buyer expects a move soon
CONVICTION SCORE (0–10)
+2 INSIDERInsider trade in same direction within 30 days (buy→call, sell→put)
+2 CONGRESSCongressional trade in same direction within 60 days
+2 GOLDEN SWEEPPremium ≥ $1M — institutional-scale bet
+2 5× VOL/OIExtremely elevated volume relative to open interest
+1 eachVol/OI ≥ 2×, out-of-the-money, DTE ≤ 14 days, premium ≥ $500K
−1 EARNINGSEarnings within 3 days — likely hedging noise, not directional
READING RESULTS
Sort any column by clicking its header. Use the filters to narrow by type, minimum score, or minimum premium. Click any row to expand it and see the underlying insider and congressional trades that contributed to the score. High-conviction signals have corroboration from multiple independent data streams.
DATA SOURCES & LIMITATIONS
Options data is from Yahoo Finance — delayed, not real-time tick data. There is no sweep detection (bid vs. ask side). Insider trades come from SEC EDGAR Form 4 filings (~1–2 day lag). Congressional trades from public STOCK Act disclosures. This is a research tool, not financial advice.
